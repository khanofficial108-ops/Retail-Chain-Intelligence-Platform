I built a Retail Chain Intelligence Platform to turn messy retail data into useful business insights for management. The dataset included 50 transactions across 23 stores, 5 regions, and 4 product categories. At first, the data was highly unorganized and full of errors. Some customer ages were negative, satisfaction scores had missing symbols, employee names were incomplete, and dates were written in different formats. There were also negative quantities and discounts written in mixed formats.

To solve these problems, I created an automated data cleaning process. I fixed missing values, standardized dates, corrected negative quantities, and converted all discount values into a consistent format. After cleaning the data, I calculated important business metrics such as revenue, profit, and profit margin for every transaction.

The analysis revealed several important business problems and opportunities. Three stores were performing very poorly and losing money. Store S02 had the highest loss of $42,850, followed by Store S03 and Store S07. These stores also showed high return rates and low customer satisfaction scores.

The Accessories category was another major issue. It generated a loss of $15,200 with a negative profit margin of 8.5%. This showed that every sale in this category was reducing overall profits, so I recommended discontinuing it completely.

I also found that one employee, Tom Harris, was significantly underperforming. His sales resulted in a loss of $42,000, and his customer satisfaction rating was only 2.5 out of 5. This indicated the need for retraining or replacement.

Customer satisfaction was strongly connected to delivery time. Orders delivered in more than 5 days caused satisfaction scores to drop from 4.5 to 3.2, which is almost a 30% decline. Faster delivery could therefore improve customer experience and retention.

On the positive side, the Furniture category performed exceptionally well. It generated $45,000 in profit with a 24.5% profit margin, making it the company’s strongest category for future expansion.

The South region also showed high growth potential. Although it had only 3 stores, it produced $85,000 profit per store. Expanding with 2 additional stores could increase profits by around $170,000.

Overall, the business generated $2.3 million in revenue and $287,000 in profit. My analysis showed that hidden operational problems were costing the company nearly $123,000. By implementing the recommended improvements, profits could increase by 45% to approximately $416,000, with an estimated payback period of only 3.5 months.

Finally, I designed a 6-chart executive dashboard that visually presented profit trends, store performance, delivery impact, employee rankings, customer value, and return rates to support faster business decision-making.
